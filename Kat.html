Being born bi-racial meant for me always thinking about how I presented myself, and who I was perceived to be.
I was fortunate to have been given two incredible, inquisitve brothers - both were and are creative, curious and contemplative.
We grew up in the suburbs, and all knew we would go to college for design.
I went to a college in downtown Chicago, and when the loans ran out I moved to New York and applied for internships.
My second internship gave me the chance to really explore the production side of fashion, and I quickly realized that this was what I wanted to contribute.
Life at some point intervened, and I had a son.
My proirities began to shift, and my career along with them.
I made the lateral move to button production, where I enjoied full autonomy of the design and production departments.
The gentrification of my neighorhood meant again having to look at my priorities, and I soon decided it was time to go home.
Through all my comings and goings the internal conversation about who I was, and what impact I was meant to have continued - and that conversation brought me here to I.C. Stars.

I chose this video because as a girl in middle school I was very shy, and could barely make eye contact. I had a teacher, her name was Ms. Anderson at the time, and she saw how shy I was and enrolled me in what she called a "Special Project".
Me and my classmate Karla were chosen, and this project was for after school, outside of our grades.
We had to choose someone to do a reasearch paper on, and then present it. I chose Marian Anderson, because she overcame adversity, was incredibly stylish and traveled the world.
As a young woman she was my idol. She also shared the last name of the teacher that changed my life.

I chose this video because the idea of Self-Care changed my life. As a working mom I carried that mom guilt with me everytime I left the house, and assuaged it at my own expense.
I would spend every single minute trying to make events and playdates when I knew I was running on fumes.
Even when things were good, I was miserable and just didn't feel like me. Hearing this TED Talk, and knowing the value of "Reconnecting with my breath" changed the way I prioritized everything, and gave me the space to be a happier mother.
